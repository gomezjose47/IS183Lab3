Portrait of Elenor of Toledo

The Portrait of Elenor of Toledo and Her Son is a painting done by the italian artist Agnola di cosnimo, known as Bronzino, finished ca 1545. One of his Most Famous works, it is housed in the Uffizi Gallery of Florence, Italy and is considered one of the preeminent examples of Mannerist.

Portrait of The Artist

Portrait of the Arist holding a Thistile is an oil painting on parchment pasted on canvas by German artist Albercht Durer, painted in 1493. It is the earliest of Durer's painted self-portraits and has been identified as one of the first self-portaraits painted by a Northern artist.

Girl with The Pearl Earring

Girl with a Pearl Earring is one of Johannes Vemeer's masterworks and, as the name implies, uses a pearl earring for a focal point. Today the painting is kept in the Mauritshuis gallery in the Hauge. It is sometimes referred to as "the Mona Lisa of the North" or the "Dutch Mona Lisa."
